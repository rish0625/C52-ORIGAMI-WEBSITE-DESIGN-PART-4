Origami Facts

History

  • Origami is the art of paper folding, which is often associated with Japanese culture.
  • In modern usage, the word "origami" is used as an inclusive term for all folding practices, regardless of their culture of origin.
  • Origami is derived from two Japanese words: Ori (folded) and Kami (paper).
  • Before that, the art was called “orikata” (folded shapes).
  • An ancient Japanese legend says if you fold one thousand cranes you will be granted a wish.

Pigeon Tutorial

The Pigeon Backstory

  • Pigeons are one of only a small number of species to pass the 'mirror test' – a test of self recognition.
  • They can also recognise each letter of the human alphabet, differentiate between photographs, and even distinguish different humans within a photograph.
  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Both female and male pigeons share responsibility of caring for and raising young.

Panda Tutorial

The Panda Life

  • Pandas have great camouflage for their environment.
  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

Flying Cicada Tutorial

The Cicada Scoop

  • Even though they spend almost 7 years of their life underground, cicadas are active their whole lives.
  • Cicadas use the aging of trees to keep track of time.
  • Math helps cicadas avoid predators.
  • There are 12 different broods of cicadas.
